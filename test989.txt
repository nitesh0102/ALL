<p><b>Rameshbabu Praggnanandhaa</b> (born 10 August 2005) is an Indian <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_(chess)" title="Grandmaster (chess)">chess grandmaster</a>. Praggnanandhaa and his sister <a href="/wiki/R_Vaishali" title="R Vaishali">Vaishali</a> are the first brother-sister duo to both earn the GM title.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> They are also the first brother-sister duo to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/Candidates_Tournament" title="Candidates Tournament">Candidates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Rameshbabu_Praggnanandhaa_in_2023_(52638512205).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Rameshbabu_Praggnanandhaa_in_2023_%2852638512205%29.jpg/220px-Rameshbabu_Praggnanandhaa_in_2023_%2852638512205%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Rameshbabu_Praggnanandhaa_in_2023_%2852638512205%29.jpg/330px-Rameshbabu_Praggnanandhaa_in_2023_%2852638512205%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Rameshbabu_Praggnanandhaa_in_2023_%2852638512205%29.jpg/440px-Rameshbabu_Praggnanandhaa_in_2023_%2852638512205%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2100" data-file-height="1400" /></a><figcaption>Praggnanandhaa <i>(right)</i> pictured with his mother, Nagalakshmi <i>(left)</i>.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Praggnanandhaa was born in <a href="/wiki/Chennai" title="Chennai">Chennai</a>, Tamil Nadu, on 10 August 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His father, Rameshbabu, works as a branch manager at <a href="/wiki/TNSC_Bank" title="TNSC Bank">TNSC Bank</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and his mother, Nagalakshmi, is a <a href="/wiki/Homemaker" class="mw-redirect" title="Homemaker">homemaker</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His elder sister, <a href="/wiki/R_Vaishali" title="R Vaishali">R Vaishali</a>, is also a chess grandmaster.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Praggnanandhaa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2013.E2.80.932017"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2017">2013–2017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Praggnanandhaa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2013–2017"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Praggnanandhaa won the <a href="/wiki/World_Youth_Chess_Championship" title="World Youth Chess Championship">World Youth Chess Championship</a> Under-8 title in 2013, earning him the title of <a href="/wiki/FIDE_Master" class="mw-redirect" title="FIDE Master">FIDE Master</a>. He won the under-10 title in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Praggnanandhaa became the youngest <a href="/wiki/International_master" class="mw-redirect" title="International master">international master</a> in history, at the age of 10 years, 10 months, and 19 days.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He achieved his first grandmaster <a href="/wiki/Norm_(chess)" title="Norm (chess)">norm</a> at the <a href="/wiki/World_Junior_Chess_Championship" title="World Junior Chess Championship">World Junior Chess Championship</a> in November 2017, finishing fourth with 8 points.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2018">2018</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Praggnanandhaa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2018"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He gained his second norm at the Heraklion Fischer Memorial GM norm tournament in Greece on 17 April 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On 23 June 2018 he achieved his third and final norm at the Gredine Open in <a href="/wiki/Urtij%C3%ABi" title="Urtijëi">Urtijëi</a>, Italy, by defeating <a href="/wiki/Luca_Moroni" title="Luca Moroni">Luca Moroni</a> in the eighth round to become, at the age of 12 years, 10 months and 13 days, the then second-youngest person ever to achieve the rank of grandmaster (<a href="/wiki/Sergey_Karjakin" title="Sergey Karjakin">Sergey Karjakin</a> attained the title at 12 years and 7 months).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He is the sixth-youngest person ever to achieve the title of <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_(chess)" title="Grandmaster (chess)">Grandmaster</a> (GM), behind <a href="/wiki/Abhimanyu_Mishra" title="Abhimanyu Mishra">Abhimanyu Mishra</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Karjakin" title="Sergey Karjakin">Sergey Karjakin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gukesh_D" title="Gukesh D">Gukesh D</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ya%C4%9F%C4%B1z_Kaan_Erdo%C4%9Fmu%C5%9F" title="Yağız Kaan Erdoğmuş">Yağız Kaan Erdoğmuş</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Javokhir_Sindarov" title="Javokhir Sindarov">Javokhir Sindarov</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Praggnanandhaa was invited to the Magistral de <a href="/wiki/Le%C3%B3n,_Spain" title="León, Spain">León</a> Masters in Spain for a four-game rapid match against <a href="/wiki/Wesley_So" title="Wesley So">Wesley So</a>. He defeated So in game one, and after three games the score was tied at 1½–1½. In the last game, So defeated Praggnanandhaa, winning the match 2½–1½.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2018, Praggnanandhaa tied for third place with GM <a href="/wiki/Alder_Escobar_Forero" title="Alder Escobar Forero">Alder Escobar Forero</a> and IM Denys Shmelov in the Charlotte Chess Center's Winter 2018 GM Norm Invitational held in <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte</a>, North Carolina, with a score of 5.0/9. <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019">2019</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Praggnanandhaa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2019"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2019, Praggnanandhaa won the <a href="/wiki/Xtracon_Chess_Open" title="Xtracon Chess Open">Xtracon Chess Open</a> in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, scoring 8½/10 points (+7–0=3).<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On 12 October 2019, he won the World Youth Championships in the Under-18 section with a score of 9/11.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In December 2019, he became the second-youngest person to achieve a rating of 2600.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He did this at the age of 14 years, 3 months and 24 days.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2021">2021</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Praggnanandhaa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2021"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2021, Praggnanandhaa won the Polgar Challenge, the first leg (out of four) of the Julius Baer Challengers Chess Tour, a rapid online event organized by Julius Baer Group and Chess24.com for young talents.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>  He scored 15.5/19, 1.5 points ahead of the next best placed competitors.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  This win helped him qualify for the next <a href="/wiki/Champions_Chess_Tour_2021" title="Champions Chess Tour 2021">Meltwater Champions Chess Tour</a> on 24 April 2021, where he finished in 10th place with a score of 7/15 (+4-5=6), including wins against <a href="/wiki/Teimour_Radjabov" title="Teimour Radjabov">Teimour Radjabov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jan-Krzysztof_Duda" title="Jan-Krzysztof Duda">Jan-Krzysztof Duda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Karjakin" title="Sergey Karjakin">Sergey Karjakin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Johan-Sebastian_Christiansen" title="Johan-Sebastian Christiansen">Johan-Sebastian Christiansen</a> as well as a draw against World Champion <a href="/wiki/Magnus_Carlsen" title="Magnus Carlsen">Magnus Carlsen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Praggnanandhaa entered the <a href="/wiki/Chess_World_Cup_2021" title="Chess World Cup 2021">Chess World Cup 2021</a> as the 90th seed. He defeated GM <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Sargissian" title="Gabriel Sargissian">Gabriel Sargissian</a> 2–0 in round 2, and advanced to round 4 after defeating GM <a href="/wiki/Micha%C5%82_Krasenkow" title="Michał Krasenkow">Michał Krasenkow</a> in the rapid tiebreaks in round 3. He was eliminated in round four by <a href="/wiki/Maxime_Vachier-Lagrave" title="Maxime Vachier-Lagrave">Maxime Vachier-Lagrave</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2022">2022</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Praggnanandhaa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2022"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Praggnanandhaa played in the Masters section of the <a href="/wiki/Tata_Steel_Chess_Tournament_2022" title="Tata Steel Chess Tournament 2022">Tata Steel Chess Tournament 2022</a>, winning games against <a href="/wiki/Andrey_Esipenko" title="Andrey Esipenko">Andrey Esipenko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vidit_Gujrathi" title="Vidit Gujrathi">Vidit Gujrathi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nils_Grandelius" title="Nils Grandelius">Nils Grandelius</a>, finishing in 12th place with a final score of 5½.
</p><p>He was part of India-2 team in <a href="/wiki/44th_Chess_Olympiad" title="44th Chess Olympiad">44th Chess Olympiad</a> which went on to finish third and won the bronze medal 
</p><p>On 20 February 2022, he became the third Indian player (after <a href="/wiki/Viswanathan_Anand" title="Viswanathan Anand">Anand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pentala_Harikrishna" title="Pentala Harikrishna">Harikrishna</a>) to win a game against World Champion <a href="/wiki/Magnus_Carlsen" title="Magnus Carlsen">Magnus Carlsen</a> in any time format, in the online <i>Airthings Masters</i> rapid tournament of the <a href="/wiki/Champions_Chess_Tour_2022" title="Champions Chess Tour 2022">Champions Chess Tour 2022</a>, with a 15+10 time control.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The record has since broken by <a href="/wiki/Gukesh_D" title="Gukesh D">Gukesh D</a>, on 16 October 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the Chessable Masters online rapid chess tournament in May 2022, he defeated Carlsen once again, his second win over him in three months, and advanced to the finals.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He also defeated Carlsen three times in the FTX Crypto Cup 2022, finishing second behind Carlsen in the final standings.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2023">2023</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Praggnanandhaa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2023"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2023, Praggnanandhaa played in the <a href="/wiki/Tata_Steel_Chess_Tournament_2023" title="Tata Steel Chess Tournament 2023">Tata Steel Chess Masters 2023</a>. He defeated a 2800-rated grandmaster, <a href="/wiki/Ding_Liren" title="Ding Liren">Ding Liren</a>, his first time playing, so in a classical game.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He ended the tournament in 9th place with a score of 6/13.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Chess_World_Cup_2023" title="Chess World Cup 2023">Chess World Cup 2023</a>, 18-year-old Praggnanandhaa became the world's youngest player to reach the <a href="/wiki/Chess_World_Cup" title="Chess World Cup">Chess World Cup</a> final, defeating<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fabiano_Caruana" title="Fabiano Caruana">Fabiano Caruana</a> in tie-breaks in the semi-final. He has also become the second Indian after <a href="/wiki/Viswanathan_Anand" title="Viswanathan Anand">Viswanathan Anand</a> to reach the final in Chess World Cup history. His play<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> against former classical World Chess Champion <a href="/wiki/Magnus_Carlsen" title="Magnus Carlsen">Magnus Carlsen</a> in the final resulted in a defeat in the rapid tie-breaks, securing him second place and qualification for the <a href="/wiki/Candidates_Tournament_2024" title="Candidates Tournament 2024">2024 Candidates Tournament</a>. He was <a href="/wiki/Second_(chess)" class="mw-redirect" title="Second (chess)">seconded</a> during that tournament by Russian grandmaster <a href="/wiki/Peter_Svidler" title="Peter Svidler">Peter Svidler</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2024">2024</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Praggnanandhaa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2024"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Praggnanandhaa placed 5th out of 8 participants in the <a href="/wiki/2024_Candidates_Tournament" class="mw-redirect" title="2024 Candidates Tournament">2024 Candidates Tournament</a>, getting 7 points out of 14. Additionally, he defeated the World number 1, Magnus Carlsen, for the first time in a classical 'over the board' match in the 3rd round of the Norway Chess 2024 tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
